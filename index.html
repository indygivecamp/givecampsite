Geeks giving back!

Every year, the Indianapolis tech/startup community gathers to help our non-profit community with their technical projects. We call it Indy GiveCamp.

Volunteer today

Who we are

Indy GiveCamp is a weekend-long collaboration between local developers, designers, database administrators, and non-profits. It is an opportunity for the technical community to celebrate and express gratitude for the contributions of these organizations by contributing code and technical know-how directly towards the needs of the groups.

When and where

November 1-3, 2013 at MID technologies 9600 Association Ct, Indianapolis, IN

Who we help

We help some amazing local non-profit organziations. They work hard all year making our communities better, so it's the least we can do to show them we appreciate their hard work. Check out this year's list below.

Get involved

Volunteer to help. Sponsor a meal. Share with a non-profit in need. There are lots of ways to help make this weekend a success. Want to know more? Check out our FAQ.

Accepted Projects

  • Hearts & Hands of Indiana

    Hearts & Hands of Indiana has a simple mission: help families and individuals attain affordable, long-term home ownership on the Near Westside of Indianapolis by providing education, mentoring, and quality rehabilitated homes. They are growing that mission, going from one new house to four this coming year! They have a decent website and great stories to tell, but they have no simple way to make changes to the site. That's where we come in.

  • Business and Professional Exchange

    Business & Professional Exchange holds weekly meetings specifically for professionals in career transition. Members learn and practice the skills needed to find jobs in an ever-changing employment environment. They've grown to six chapters across the state based on the quality of their organization but feel limited in their reach without a professional-looking website.

  • Pathway to Recovery

    The mission of Pathway to Recovery is to provide housing with wrap around services to homeless adults with co-existing disorders such as mental illness, substance abuse, and other barriers that prevent them from accessing and sustaining mainstream housing opportunities. Pathway helps participants end their cycle of homelessness by becoming mentally and physically stable, financially self-sufficient, and begin healthy independent living following placement in safe affordable substance-free permanent housing. They recently had a volunteer convert their site to a cms. This is great, but they could use help in learning how to use it!

  • Lebanon Boys and Girls Club

    The Lebanon Area Boys and Girls Club is working to help youth of all backgrounds develop the qualities needed to become responsible citizens and leaders. We offer daily access to a broad range of programs in five core program areas including Character and Leadership Development; Education and Career Development; Health and Life Skills; The Arts; and Sports, Fitness and Recreation and several specialized initiatives. All programs are designed to drive positive, measurable outcomes for youth and reinforce necessary life skills.
